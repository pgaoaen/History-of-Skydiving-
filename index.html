The History of

Skydiving

Skydiving can be traced as far back as the early 1100’s and is still practiced in todays modern era. Daredevils from all walks of life have experimented on various and extreme ways to fly. Learn about important moments in skydiving history through a collection of shared stories found right here on the internet. Scroll on!

1100s

The concept of falling from the sky was first recorded by Chinese historian Sima Qian, who shared a peculiar story of an Emperor who survived a high jump from a burning building after using a parachute that was haphazardly put together.

1200s

Since then there have been a plehora of stories in which people used large conical hats and even umbrellas to jump off tall buildings.

1485

Leonardo DaVinci sketched the blueprints for the first parachute with his accompanying description, "if a man have a tent made of linen of which the apertures (openings) have all been stopped up, and it be 23 ft. across and 12 ft. in depth, he will be able to throw himself down from any great height without suffering any injury."

1933

Skydiving was heavily used in the military after the development of parachute technology. The act of skydiving was used as a tactical move in war settings. The soviets were the first to stage a mass drop of 62 soldiers in 1933.

1952

After the war, skydiving became much more popular within the community of paratroopers. Many returning soldiers participated in regular competitions, which led skydiving into a national sport in 1952.

2012

The highest recorded skydive in history happened on October 14th 2012 when 43 year old Austrian skydiver Felix Baumgartner successfully jumped 128,000 feet from the edge of space. He free falled for a total of four minutes and 20 seconds at the supersonic speed of 834 mph!

2016

The most daring drop in skydiving history occured on July 30, 2016 when Luke Alkins became the first skydiver to jump out of a plane without a parachute or wingsuit and lived to share the story with world!
